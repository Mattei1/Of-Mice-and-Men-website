Characters

Lennie- Large migrant worker with an intellectual disability. He's dependant of George and both of them have a shared dream that consists of owning their own farm full with rabbits and mice.

George- A small man that takes care of Lennie. Although he manipulates Lennie, he still cares for him and defends him.

Candy- Old ranch worker. He fears that the ranch boos will kill him because he's no longer useful. He gives all his life's savings to George so the could join them on their dream. His dog, Carlson got shot in the head, foreshadowing the death of Lennie in the end.

Curley's Wife- Only known as the wife of Curley, she's the only woman in the novel. Curley's Wife portrayed as a victim because men in the ranch call her a "tramp", a "tart" and a "looloo".

Curley- The boss's son, a small, agrresive man that picks fights with bigger men to assert his dominance and feel more confident. He also is very jealous and insecure of his wife.

Crooks- Crooks, the black stable-hand, named after his crooked back. Victim of racism due to his skin color. Asks Lennie if he can join him and George on their dream, the stable.

Slim-A skilled mule driver acknowledged as the “prince” of the ranch. Only character who seems to be at peace with himself. Insightful man, often seeked for advice,understands nature of the bond between George and Lennie, and comforts George at the book’s tragic ending.

Carlson- A ranch's hand who complained about Candy's dog being old. He offers himself to kill the dog without him suffering. The same gun he killed the dog with, is the gun George used to kill Lennie.

The Boss- Curley's dad. He is a well dressed man whose name is never mentioned. It is mentioned that he delivered a gallon of whiskey

Aunt Clara- Kind woman who gifted Lennie mice and took care of him until her death. In the end she appears as a vision chastising Lennie for causing George trouble.
